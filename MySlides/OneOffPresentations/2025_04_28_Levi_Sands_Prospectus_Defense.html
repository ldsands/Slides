## Theoretical Background - Markets - Change in markets and as the focal point for contentious social interactions (Fligstein and Dauter 2007; Haedicke 2016; King and Pearce 2010) - Sociocognitive categories as common understandings within a market (Khaire and Wadhwani 2010) - For example what should be considered "meat" - The establishment of legitimacy of new products and organization practices or goals (Lee, Hiatt, and Lounsbury 2017; Vasi 2007) - Field Theory (also called a Theory of Fields) - Based on Fligstein and McAdam (2011; 2012) - Strategic Action Fields (SAF) in this case the meat market - Incumbents who try to maintain dominant position in SAF - Challengers who try to "break into" the market - Governance Units who manage field rules - Contribution to literature - Adding Incumbent and Challenger "Aligned" organizations - Describing differences from incumbents and their "uses" - Adding example of challenger response to incumbent actions
