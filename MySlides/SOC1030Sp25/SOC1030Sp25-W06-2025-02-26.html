## Social Reproduction of Inequality - **Social Reproduction of Inequality**: The basic idea is that social positions (e.g., social class, occupations, education, etc.) tend to pass on from one generation to another - **Social Institution**: institutions are social structures that have rules, norms and boundaries that influence the behavior of individuals. - Examples: Families, religion, government, schools, healthcare etc. - **Social Capital**: connections to people who have resources you can access - Examples: law or health expertise (doctors, lawyers), job prospects - **Cultural Capital**: class based knowledge and preferences used to signal your class to others - Examples: Opera (RBG), SNL Colbert etc., cars, etc. - **Social Closure**: group boundaries and identities that prevent others from using group resources - Examples: Country clubs, LDS humanitarian aid, Federalist Society etc.

## Race VS Ethnicity - **Ethnicity**: "a social category of people who share a common culture" (Andersen, Taylor, and Logio 2017) - "Ethic groups have a consciousness of their common cultural bond" - **Race**: "a group treated as distinct in society based on certain characteristics" (Andersen, Taylor, and Logio 2017) - Race is not a biological classification but a socially constructed classification

## Racialization - **Racialization**:The process of assigning people a race, along with the personality, behavioral, and social characteristics associated with that race, based on physical or cultural cues (skin color or speech patterns). - Formal laws and social institutions (from top down) - Media and public opinions (from bottom up)
## Race and Social Construction - What does it mean that race is a socially constructed concept? - The boundaries between the racial groups change over time. - Traits in physical appearance may not always be the marker of a racial group. - Race is real with real social consequences: The dominant group receives social or economic advantages while the oppressed group are worse off! - Race is a social construction, that is that people in the same society share a common understanding about their relationship on the basis of some assumptions about reality.
