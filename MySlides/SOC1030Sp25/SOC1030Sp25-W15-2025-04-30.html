## Is Social Media Polarizing - **Illusory Truth Effect**: "[...] tendency to believe false information to be correct after repeated exposure" ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illusory_truth_effect)) - **Continued Influence Effect**: "[...] the tendency for misinformation to continue influencing people's thinking and decision-making even after it has been corrected or debunked." ([Jason Hreha](https://www.thebehavioralscientist.com/glossary/continued-influence-effect)) - **Confirmation Bias**: the tendency to look for, prefer, interpret, and remember information that confirms their current beliefs (Nickerson 1998) - **Cognitive Dissonance**: "the mental disturbance people feel when they realize their cognitions and actions are inconsistent or contradictory" [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_dissonance) - **Motivated Reasoning**: uses emotionally biased reasoning to produce justifications or make decisions that are most desired rather than those that accurately reflect the evidence, while still reducing cognitive dissonance - **Echo Chamber**: a media environment in which information and beliefs that confirm prior beliefs are amplified - [They're lying to you. Here's what to do about it. - Johnny Harris](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yWgG3Mgn2Gc) (Required video)
Illusory Truth Effect and Continued Influence Effect, both concepts influence what information we use to develop our beliefs
Confirmation bias impacts what information we integrate into or reinforce our beliefs

Cognitive dissonance and motivated reasoning are key to understanding why people will defend their beliefs even (and perhaps especially) in the face on contradictory evidence

All these concepts are encouraged on social media platforms, creating echo chambers which encourage polarization
