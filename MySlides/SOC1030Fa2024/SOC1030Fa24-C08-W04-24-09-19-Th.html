## Social Mobility - Social mobility is the movement between different positions within a system of social stratification in any given society. - **Horizontal mobility**: a group or individual transitioning from one social status to another situated more or less on the same rung of the ladder. - **Vertical mobility**: the rise or fall of an individual or group from one social stratum to another. - **Intergenerational mobility**: one generation moves up or down in social class compared with their previous generation. - **Intragenerational mobility**: an individual moves up or down in social class within their own lifetime. - **Economic mobility** is the ability to move up and down the economic ladder during one's lifetime and across generations. [(Pew Trusts)](https://www.pewtrusts.org/en/research-and-analysis/data-visualizations/2013/faces-of-economic-mobility) - **Absolute mobility** looks at whether individuals exceed their parents' family income or wealth as adults. [(Pew Trusts)](https://www.pewtrusts.org/en/research-and-analysis/data-visualizations/2013/faces-of-economic-mobility) - **Relative mobility** assesses individuals' rank on the economic ladder compared with their parents. [(Pew Trusts)](https://www.pewtrusts.org/en/research-and-analysis/data-visualizations/2013/faces-of-economic-mobility)

## How Do Sociologists Study Mobility? - A mobility table is a two-way contingency table in which the individuals are classified according to origins and destinations. - Job examples: - Upper-nonmanual: lawyers, engineers; doctors - Lower-nonmanual: customer representatives; sales person; bank teller - Upper-manual: electricians, Carpenters, - Lower-manual: General Laborers, janitors, assembly line workers

## What is poverty? - **Poverty** a condition of deprivation due to economic circumstances that are severe enough that the individual cannot live with dignity in his or her society. - **Poverty line** (poverty threshold) - estimated food cost per household size, multiplied by 3. Household with income below this line is considered “poor.” - **Severe poverty**: income less than half of federal poverty line

## Experiment: Moving To Opportunity - A housing voucher plan to help 7,100 families in public housing in 1990s https://www.hud.gov/programdescription/mto - Baltimore, Boston, Chicago, Los Angeles, and New York City - The goal was to disperse at least 75 percent of the families into neighborhoods with less than 30 percent minority residents. - One-third received no housing voucher at all, - One third received vouchers with no restrictions on where they moved, - The final group—the treatment group—received the vouchers and assistance in relocating to rental units in areas with less than 10 percent poverty rates plus tutorials in basic life skills ranging from balancing a checkbook to yard work to lease negotiation.
