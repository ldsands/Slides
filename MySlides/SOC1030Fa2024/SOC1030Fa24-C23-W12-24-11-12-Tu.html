## Progress in International Treaties - The Kyoto Protocol: adopted on December 11th, 1997, in force on February 16th, 2005 - 1st commitment period 2008 to 2012 - 5% reduction against 1990 level - 2nd commitment period 2013 to 2020 - 18% reduction against 1990 level - Mixed results - The Paris Agreement aims to substantially reduce global greenhouse gas emissions to limit the global temperature - Goal of increase in this century to no more than 2 degrees Celsius above preindustrial levels - The agreement: commitments from all major emitting countries - Countries agreed cut their climate-altering pollution and to strengthen those commitments over time - 197 countries—every nation on earth, with the last signatory being war-torn Syria—have adopted the Paris Agreement, Of those, 179 have solidified their climate proposals with formal approval, including the U.S. - US left in 2020 and rejoined in 2021
## Some other Potential Solutions - More International Agreements and Treaties - Changing the payoffs facing individuals and firms - Forcing firms to pay their share of cost of these externalities that they produce - Governmental incentives to encourage public and private consumptions (e.g. public transportation and private cars) - Carbon pricing (cap-and-trade, carbon tax) - Common Pool Resource management and regulation - Non-profit organizations in place of for-profit corporations
